Pancit Canton

★ ★ ★ ★ ★
4.3 (2,456 reviews)

Pancit Canton is a popular Filipino noodle dish made with stir-fried wheat noodles, vegetables, and meat. Easy one-pot dish that's ready in minutes. This noodle stir-fry features a hearty and tasty mix of crisp pork belly and veggies. Perfect for family dinners or special occasions!

Prep Time
25 min
Cook Time
20 min
Servings
6-8

About This Dish

Pancit comes from the Hokkien phrase 'pian i sit,' meaning 'something conveniently cooked fast.' Chinese traders introduced noodles to the Philippines, and Filipinos adapted the dish with local ingredients and flavors. Today, pancit is served at birthdays and celebrations as a symbol of longevity.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pancit canton noodles (or thin egg noodles)
  • 1/2 lb chicken breast, sliced thin
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cups cabbage, shredded
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1/2 cup celery, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Calamansi or lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

1

Soak the pancit canton noodles in water for about 10 minutes until slightly softened. Drain and set aside.

2

Heat oil in a large wok or pan over high heat. Sauté garlic and onions until fragrant.

3

Add chicken and cook until no longer pink, about 5 minutes.

4

Add shrimp and cook until they turn pink, about 2-3 minutes.

5

Add all vegetables (carrot, cabbage, snow peas, celery) and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes.

6

Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chicken broth. Bring to a simmer.

7

Add the drained noodles and toss everything together, cooking until the noodles absorb the liquid and become tender, about 5-7 minutes.

8

Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with calamansi or lemon wedges on the side.

Pro Tips

  • 💡 Don't oversoak the noodles - they should still have some firmness as they'll continue cooking in the pan.
  • 💡 Use high heat for stir-frying to get that nice 'wok hei' flavor.
  • 💡 You can substitute or add other proteins like pork, Chinese sausage, or tofu.
  • 💡 You can substitute or add other proteins like pork, Chinese sausage, or tofu.
    • 💡 Slice the pork belly into thin strips to cook quickly. Brown in the pan until it renders fat and is fully cooked.
  • 💡 Squeeze calamansi or lemon over the pancit before eating for added flavor.

Cultural Significance

In Filipino culture, noodles symbolize long life, which is why pancit is traditionally served at birthday celebrations. The practice of sharing pancit also represents the Filipino value of community and togetherness.
